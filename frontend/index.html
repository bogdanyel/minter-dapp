Meet the Planets


Let us introduce you to the types of planets you can discover in our collection.



Hycean


A hypothetical type of habitable planet described as a hot, water-covered planet with a hydrogen-rich atmosphere.

Terrestrial


A planet that is composed primarily of carbonaceous or silicate rocks or metals.

Gas Giant


A massive planet composed primarily of hydrogen and helium.

Carbon


A theoretical terrestrial planet that could form if protoplanetary discs are carbon-rich and oxygen-poor.

Desert


A theoretical terrestrial planet with very little water.

Gas Dwarf


A low-mass planet composed primarily of hydrogen and helium.

Chthorian


An extrasolar planet that orbits close to its parent star.

Helium


A theoretical planet that may form via mass loss from a low-mass dwarf.

Ice Giant


A theoretical planet with an icy surface and consists of a global cryosphere.

Poison Giant


A planet whose atmosphere is inhospitable to all life forms.

Ocean Planet


A theoretical planet which has a substantial fraction of its mass made of water.
